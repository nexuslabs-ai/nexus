Phase A · playground · light-mode tone identity

Two directions for light-mode tones.

Baked = exactly what's committed to the spec (keep the Tailwind-family hues; tones differ by intensity). Hue-spread = the stronger-separation option we set aside (give each tone its own hue + a bit more chroma, so they're distinct colors). Flip between them, flip light/dark, and re-tune the strength. The card readouts show the live hue · lightC · darkC.

1.00

Baked — keep hues, differ by intensity

Tone identities stay (slate/zinc/gray are the familiar cool greys; stone warm; neutral grey). Light reads as warm-paper vs cool-paper. The catch: slate ≈ zinc ≈ gray in light (same ~263° hue, just more/less of it). Lowest risk; matches Tailwind lineage.

Hue-spread — each tone its own color

Every tone is distinguishable at a glance in light (blue / teal / violet / amber / grey). But it redefines zinc (→violet) and gray (→teal), diverging further from Tailwind, and pushes more chroma onto near-white (watch for dinginess at high strength).

Illustrative OKLCH replicating the spec's `deriveSurfaces` shape at `TONE_CONTRAST=60` (paper L≈0.987, light tint = lightC·(1+(1−L)·1.4), dark = darkC). Baked values are the exact committed `SURFACE_TONE`. If hue-spread wins, it reopens the Q6 tone-identity decision (and changes the tone meanings) — a bigger call than the tint calibration; this is a prototype to see it, not a commitment.
