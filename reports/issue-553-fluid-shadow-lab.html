Nexus Surfaces vs Pasted Surfaces

The useful thing to borrow is not the exact surface names. Nexus already has semantic surfaces. The thing worth stealing is the way elevation is rendered differently in light and dark.

Light mode takeaway shadow carries elevation

The pasted light surfaces are nearly flat. That is why the shadow stack matters: it separates cards without making the whole page look grey.

Dark mode takeaway highlight plus ring

The pasted dark recipe avoids muddy black blobs. It uses a tiny top highlight, a faint outline, and layered drops so elevation is visible on dark surfaces.

Recommendation: do not hardcode Fluid's surface colors into Nexus. Keep Nexus semantic derived surfaces, then explore a Fluid-style shadow renderer that can emit the light and dark recipes shown here in follow-up issue #556.
